Scooby-Doo, Where Are You!

Scooby-Doo, Where Are You! is an American animated comedy television series produced by Hanna-Barbera for CBS and developed by Joe Ruby and Ken Spears. The series debuted on September 13, 1969, as part of the network's Saturday morning animation lineup, and ran for two seasons until October 31, 1970. A selection of episodes from the subsequent animated programmes Scooby-Doo's All-Star Laff-A-Lympics and The Scooby-Doo Show were shown on ABC in 1978 under the title Scooby-Doo, Where Are You! and were included in a DVD package promoted as its third season.
The series chronicles the exploits of adolescents Fred, Daphne, Velma, and Shaggy, as well as a dog named Scooby-Doo. They travel aboard The Mystery Machine van, encountering and solving mysteries. When the mystery is solved, the gang usually realises that the offender is a disguised individual who intends to profit from a local tradition or myth.
